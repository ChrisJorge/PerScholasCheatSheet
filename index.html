Key Terminology

Project

An Individual or collaborative enterpries that is carefully planned to achieve a particular outcome

  • Specific
  • Requires one-off performance
  • Requires planning
  • Large enough to need control mechanisms in place
  • Is not a business as usual activity

Brownfield Project

  • System conversion

Greenfield project

  • New implementation

Gantt Chart

Visual tool that shows what must be done and when it should be done by

Project Management

Required to ensure effective management of all activites, ensuring sucessful project completion.

  • On Time
  • Within Budget
  • Appropriate Quality

Project Manager

Responsible for managing the process and the activities of project management.

  • Planning all activities
  • Overseeing all execution
  • Monitoring progress
  • Reporting to stakeholders

Project Charter

Document that provides formal approval to proceed as a project

  • Scope
  • Objective
  • Measurments and assumptions
  • Restrictions
  • Key risks
  • Major participants
  • Project authorities
  • Expected or approved costs
  • Timeframes - Deliveries and milestones
  • Expectations of the quality of deliverables

Project Schedule

Used for documenting and detailing key milestones (defined points showing project progress)

  • Can be displayed as a Gantt Chart or list
  • When time arrives, progress will be measured and compared against milestone

Issue Register

A database or document that contains the details of issues

  • Impact of the issue.
  • who "owns" the issue.
  • Estimated resolution time.

Phases and Areas Of Interest

Planning

Determine the viability of the investment, obtain an agreement to pay, and plan the control of the project

  1. Determine viability: will the final product create enough value to return on investment?
  2. Get project charter signed off to have organizational commitment to support and pay
  3. Plan out project activites to ensure project is going to be controllable

Excecution

Plan the specifics of the project, and perform all activities to complete a successful project

  1. Collect/plan specific requirments to understand deliverables
  2. Determine/acquire resources needed to accomplish delivery
  3. Create a schematic or blueprint to detail the build
  4. Create product or prototype
  5. Test to ensure quality and functionality specified by customer
  1. Validate project will not harm environment or other existing products
  2. Ensure customers/users are prepared to recieve product
  3. Provide a knowledge transfer plan for implementation to execute
  4. Turn over all implementable components to the release process

Implementation

Go live with the project work product

  1. Perform all activites necessary to place the product into the live environment
  2. Execute a knowledge transfer plan to ensure support personnel are ready to support the end-user
  3. Notify the customer/user community as to the availability of the new product or service
  4. Provide support to operations staff and user community as the service is readied for actual usage

Closure

Complete the documentation to close out the project

  1. Finalize documentation for future needs
  2. Pay all outstanding invoices
  3. Validate customer acceptance and satisfaction
  4. Perform additional activites required to close out the project

Nine Areas Of Attention

Scope

Defines the work to be done- How much work and all expectations - Quality, Cost, Delivery, etc.

Schedule

Control document defining timing for all project activites, including delivery.

Cost

Expected and actual cost.

Quality

Evaluating the quality of the deliverables as they are being designed, built, and delivered

Risks

Something that might happen (positive or negative)

Issues

Something that is happening or has happened (always negative)

Stakeholders

Who is impacted by the project? Has the group of "affected" changed?

Administration

Reports, Paperwork, Meetings, Communication, and Purchases

Approvals

Initial, Milestone, Completion (acceptance) etc

Risks Vs Issues

Issues (Risk that has happened or is happening)

  • Focused on the present.
  • Always negative.
  • Documented in Issue Register
  • Response will be "issue work-around" if not correctable

Risks (Something that might happen)

  • Evaluate for future.
  • Positive or negative
  • Documented in Risk Register
  • Response performed based on "risk response planning".

Stakeholders

Stakeholder

An entity with an interest in the outcome of a project

  • Will receive a benefit from the project?
  • Could interrupt the project?
  • providing funding for the project?
  • operate the project "work product" when complete?
  • Involved in project delivery activites?

Customer

Entity that pays for a service or product. Has the authority to determine how "valuable" the service or product is

End-user / User

Entity that consumes / utilizes a service or product

  • Usually entails end-user achieving some level of productivity to allow customer's business to achieve its goal
  • May or not be the customer

Supplier

Entity that provides products and services used in the creation or delivery of products or services to the end customer

  • Hardware
  • Software
  • Consulting services
  • Other products
